History of Modern Roller Derby

Modern Roller derby got it's start in the early 2000s in Austin, Texas with the Texas Rollergirls.
Traditional roller derby was played on a banked track but these were expensive and difficult to
store and assemble and also limited venue options so the flat track set up was created to save time and money.
The current flat track set up is the most popular version of roller derby today.
The Early league formed what was known as the United Leagues coalition in 2004 which eventually became known as
Womens Flat Track Derby Association(WFTDA)

The Different Rule Sets

The most common rules sets are USA Roller Sports or USARS, Modern American Derby Endeavor or MADE, and The Women's Flat Track Derby Association or the WFTDA
WFTDA is by far the most popular rule set with the association having over 400 members leagues world wide.

Women's Flat Track Derby Association

The WFTDA is the most wide spread Derby Association housing over 400 members league across the World.
Each year they hold championships to decide who will take home the Hydra, Named in honor of Hydra, a founding member of the WFTDA and the first President of the association

How roller derby is played

Roller derby is played on either a flat or banked track. There are 5 members of each team present on the track, 1 Jammer, 1 Pivot and 4 blockers.
The Pivot is the leader of the pack and wears a striped helmet cover. The pivot can also become the Jammer if the the Star Cover is legally passed to her by the current jammer.
The Jammer is the point scorer and wears a star helmet cover.
The blockers do not wear any helmet covers, their primary job is to prevent the other team's jammer from scoring point while assisting their own jammer.
The game is 1 hour and if broken up in to 2 30 minutes periods and each period is broken in to 2 minute jams.
Jams can end before the 2 minutes is up, such as if the lead jammer calls off the jam, but they will ever be longer than the 2 minutes
The first Jammer to legally leave the pack becomes the lead jammer and hold the ability to end the jam whenever she wants.
She can lose the this by commiting a penatly or voluntarily removing her helmet cover, in which case the jam will last the complete 2 minutes.
The jamer earns a point for each opposing blocker she passes, her hips having passed the hips of the other players.
